Fundamental Rights in the Indian Constitution
## Fundamental Rights (Part III of the Indian Constitution)

Articles 12-35 guarantee essential rights to all citizens. These rights are justiciable, meaning they can be enforced by the Supreme Court (Article 32) and High Courts (Article 226).

Key Rights:
• Right to Equality (Art 14-18): Equality before law, prohibition of discrimination.
• Right to Freedom (Art 19-22): Speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession.
• Right against Exploitation (Art 23-24): Prohibition of human trafficking and child labor.
• Right to Freedom of Religion (Art 25-28): Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice, and propagation of religion.
• Cultural and Educational Rights (Art 29-30): Protection of interests of minorities.
• Right to Constitutional Remedies (Art 32): The "heart and soul" of the Constitution — Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.
